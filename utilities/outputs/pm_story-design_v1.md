# Omnigenesis: The Memory - Story Design

## Core Narrative

**Premise:** The universe remembers everything that ever was, is, or could be. You are the **Witness** - a consciousness drifting through infinite cosmic memory, experiencing echoes of countless worlds, civilizations, and phenomena.

**Goal:** There is no end. Only deeper. The memory is infinite - every discovery opens doors to ten more. You are not trying to finish. You are trying to **see**.

**Tone:** Endless wonder. Cosmic curiosity. The joy of infinite discovery.

**Key Shift:** The universe isn't dying - it's **dreaming**. And dreams have no end.

---

## The Witness

You don't create. You **remember**.

The primordial elements are seeds of infinite possibility. When you combine them, you're not making something new - you're triggering a memory. The universe shows you what once was, what is, what could be.

> *"The universe remembers everything. You are learning to listen."*

The deeper you go, the stranger the memories become. First you remember fire and water. Then you remember civilizations. Then you remember gods. Then you remember things that have no name.

---

## Story Structure: Infinite Depths

The story unfolds across expanding "Depths" - layers of cosmic memory. Each Depth represents deeper, stranger territories. **There is no final Depth.**

Each Depth has:
- **3-5 Story-Critical Elements** (milestones with image/video generation)
- **Lore fragments** that hint at infinite mystery
- **A gateway to the next Depth**

### Depth I: The Primordial Silence
*"Before everything, there was potential."*

## Milestone vs Regular Elements

### Milestone Elements (Image + Video + Lore)
- ~15-20 total across the game
- Trigger Imagen 4 for dramatic artwork
- Auto-trigger Veo evolution video (first discovery)
- Unlock substantial lore fragment
- Visual fanfare: screen darkens, memory "floods in"
- Sound: swelling ambient music

### Regular Elements (Text + Emoji only)
- Hundreds possible
- Instant response from Gemini Flash
- Brief description
- Optional "Evolve" button for video generation
- No image generation (keeps pace fast)

---

## UX During 9-Second Image Generation

When a milestone is discovered, the wait becomes **the experience**:

### Phase 1: Recognition (0-2s)
- Screen dims, colors desaturate
- Text: *"A memory stirs..."*
- Particles begin swirling from the two combined elements
- Soft ambient tone begins

### Phase 2: Anticipation (2-5s)
- Blurred silhouette of the image begins forming
- Lore snippet appears letter by letter
- Sound builds
- Element history shows: "Stone → Earth → Mountain → Civilization"

### Phase 3: Revelation (5-9s)
- Image sharpens progressively
- Full lore text revealed
- Sound reaches crescendo
- Final image displays with subtle glow effect

### Phase 4: Aftermath (post-load)
- "Evolve" button pulses (auto-triggers for first discovery)
- Element added to Memory collection
- Progress indicator updates (Age progress)

---

## Lore Delivery Mechanisms

### 1. Memory Fragments (Milestone discoveries)
Long-form lore (2-4 sentences) revealing universe history.

### 2. Whispers (Regular discoveries)
One-line poetic observations.
> *"Steam remembers being water. It dreams of clouds."*

### 3. The Archive
Collection screen where all discovered lore can be re-read. Organized by Age.

### 4. Loading Hints
During any wait, random hints appear:
- Combination hints: *"Longing seeks completion. What does silence long for?"*
- Lore teasers: *"The universe had a name once. It forgot."*
- Gameplay tips: *"Some elements can only be remembered in a specific order."*

---

## Sound Design Direction

### Ambient Base
- Deep, slow cosmic drone
- Filtered through reverb (sounds "distant")
- Occasional crystalline tones

### Combination Sounds
- Regular: Soft chime + whoosh
- Milestone: Building resonance, like a bell being struck in slow motion

### Age Transitions
- When entering a new Age, the ambient shifts
- Age I: Silence with occasional tones
- Age II: Organic sounds emerge (water, growth)
- Age III: Distant voices, music fragments
- Age IV: Dissonance, tension
- Age V: Return to silence, but warmer

## Key Design Principles

1. **Every element has a soul.** Even "Steam" should feel meaningful.

2. **The wait is the experience.** Don't hide loading - make it dramatic.

3. **Wonder, not completion.** The joy is in discovery, not finishing.

4. **Player is the Witness.** Everything is framed as memory, not creation.

5. **Always deeper.** Every answer reveals ten new questions. Infinity is the feature.
