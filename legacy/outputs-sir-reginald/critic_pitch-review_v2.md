The pitch plan has a good section on "What Was Hard" at the end (lines 183-190), but it's in an appendix-style position. This is valuable differentiator content that could be elevated.

## Remaining Issues (If Any)

### Ranked by Severity:

**1. Impact Stories Could Hit Harder (Medium)**
The Sarah/Marcus/David stories are good but safe. They describe near-misses and inconveniences. For maximum impact, consider:
- More visceral language about what COULD have happened
- Or a real statistic that grounds it: "Sarah's neighbor lost two fingers last year - same situation"

The stories feel workshopped. They work, but they don't punch.

**2. Demo 2 is the Longest But Least Dramatic (Low-Medium)**
45 seconds for visual diagnosis is a lot. The safety save (Demo 1) has drama - will the AI catch it in time? Material ID (Demo 3) has surprise - it knows what this is! Visual diagnosis is... useful but not exciting.

Consider: Can Demo 2 be shortened to 30s, or made more dramatic? What if the print is actively failing on camera and the AI catches it in progress?

**3. No Explicit Fallback Plan (Low)**
The pitch assumes everything works. What's the recovery if the safety demo doesn't trigger? Judges have seen plenty of failed live demos. A one-liner acknowledgment ("We've tested this 50 times, but if live demo gods aren't smiling...") could add credibility.

## The "24/7 Shop Mentor" Analogy

**Verdict: Strong**

This analogy works because:
1. **Immediate clarity** - No explanation needed
2. **Emotional resonance** - People remember learning from mentors
3. **Correct scope** - Mentors guide, they don't do the work for you
4. **Hackathon-appropriate** - No competitor shade, just aspiration

The extended version (lines 98-111 in pitch plan) is well-crafted:
> "The experienced peer who's always watching. The workshop instructor who never goes home. The mentor you wish you had, available 24/7."

Three variations of the same idea - good for different contexts.

**One concern:** The word "mentor" implies teaching/growth. Safety Monitor is more "guardian" than "mentor." But this is minor - the combined package (safety + troubleshooting) earns the mentor framing.

**Overall Demo Strategy: 7.5/10**

The demos prove three different capabilities, which is good. But Demo 2 is the weak link - it's the longest demo but the least memorable moment.

**Suggestion:** If possible, make Demo 2 a real-time catch. That's more impressive than post-mortem analysis.

## Final Verdict

**Status:** READY TO BUILD

**Confidence:** High

The v2 revisions successfully addressed all five v1 issues. The documents are now tight, focused, and hackathon-ready. The remaining issues are execution concerns (demo timing, story punch) rather than strategic problems.

### What to Focus on During Build Phase:

1. **Nail the Safety Save demo** - This is your "holy shit" moment. It must work reliably. Test it 100 times. Have a backup trigger phrase if the AI misses the visual cue.

2. **Tighten Demo 2** - Either shorten it or make it more dramatic. Real-time diagnosis > post-mortem analysis.

3. **Practice the 3-minute timing** - 105 seconds of live demo is aggressive. Know exactly where you can cut if demos run long.

4. **Have a demo failure recovery plan** - One sentence acknowledging the live demo risk can turn a failure into a relatable moment.

5. **Consider elevating the "What Was Hard" section** - Sub-500ms latency for safety-critical intervention is a genuine technical achievement. Judges should hear this explicitly, not find it in an appendix.

### If You Had More Revision Time (You Don't Need It):

- Punch up the impact stories with more visceral language
- Consider restructuring Demo 2 to be a live catch rather than a static analysis
- Add a one-line demo failure acknowledgment
